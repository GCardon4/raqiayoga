Passive Static Yoga

  1. Home
  2. Passive Static Yoga

Passive static yoga styles offer a more leisurely and focused way of practicing, allowing for greater introspection, deep relaxation, strengthening and flexibility of the body, promoting an overall balance between body and mind.

These styles are ideal for people seeking a gentler practice, focused on relaxation and restoration, as well as those who wish to complement more active activities with a calmer and more reflective approach.

-YIN YOGA More stretches!

The perfect complement to yang. Yin yoga is a relaxed style ideal for eliminating tension, as it works on FASCIA AND CONNECTIVE TISSUE.

They are sitting postures and the stretches go to the rhythm of breathing for periods between 2 and 5 minutes, this being a kind of meditation.

If your yoga practice and your life are very “YANG”, energetic and with a lot of activity, you can complement with this style to balance the internal and external energies of the body and allow both to flow freely.

Who is it for: For people looking for deep relaxation, flexibility, stress reduction and a practice that complements more dynamic or intense activities. It is especially suitable for high-performance athletes, with intense sports workloads, those who want to work on joint mobility, work on deep stretches, improve circulation, drain lactic acid and find a balance between body and mind, feminine and masculine “YIN YANG”.

- HATHA YOGA Traditional Yogic Practice

It is a traditional yogic style, since its origins it is physical yoga, which allows an experience of connection through postures between the union of the body, mind and spirit.

If you are starting yoga, HATHA is an ideal way to start. The fluid and slow movements accompanied by the rhythm of your breathing will make you connect with the practice naturally.

- YOGA NIDRA ¡More relaxing!

Known as the yoga of dreams. Yoga NIDRA is a type of yoga that invites physical and mental relaxation through conscious meditation. It is perfect for those who have lack of sleep, stress, anxiety and physical exhaustion

It provides peace and connection with oneself, it is healing and restorative, we relax our mind and our senses. It is done in quiet spaces and the postures are comfortable, static and soft.

- YOGA KIDS ¡Children growing in balance and awareness!

Special classes for children between 4 and 12 years old where the objective is for the child to learn a way to relate and connect with their body, manage their breathing and thus the ability to connect with their mind and their emotions

Yoga, with breathing and postures or asanas, helps children develop their concentration, memory, mindfulness and relaxation.

- RESTORATIVE THERAPEUTIC YOGA Physical recovery and restoration!

This practice involves the use of passive postures held for prolonged periods with the support of physical elements “PROPS” such as bolsters, belts, canes, blankets, chairs and blocks to help the body fully restore itself.

Who is it for: This type of yoga is for anyone seeking to reduce physical stress caused by high loads of sports stress, relieve physical and mental tension, and promote deep relaxation of muscles and tissues. It is especially beneficial for people with hectic lifestyles, those looking to recover from injuries, or those who want to balance more intense yoga practices with calming and restorative sessions.
