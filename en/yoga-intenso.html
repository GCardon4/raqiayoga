Power styles of yoga tend to be more intense and physically challenging compared to other styles.

These practices are ideal for those looking for a more physically and mentally demanding approach, improving endurance, muscle strength and enjoying more intense exercise while maintaining the benefits of integral Yoga practice.

Bikram yoga, also known as HOT YOGA or yoga with heat, is a variant created by Bikram Choudhury in the 1970s based on the techniques of HATHA, traditional yoga.

It is done in a room at 40 degrees temperature.

The sessions last 90 minutes and consist of a succession of 26 postures or asanas, as well as two breathing exercises at the beginning and end of the practice. Its movements work on concentration, self-control and balance, making it a very complete workout. Although the postures are not the most complex in yoga, they do require a lot of physical and mental strength to cope with the thermal demands of Bikram.

In addition to enjoying all the advantages of yoga in general, Bikram stands out for its effectiveness in eliminating toxins thanks to sweating and strengthens the immune system by performing the practice under temperatures that simulate feverish states where the body enhances states of restoration and regeneration. cellular. Likewise, heat helps to gain flexibility and sharpens mental states of concentration, which favors the conscious experience of the practitioner.
