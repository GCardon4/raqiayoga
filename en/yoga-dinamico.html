Passive static yoga styles offer a more leisurely and focused way of practicing, allowing for greater introspection, deep relaxation, strengthening and flexibility of the body, promoting an overall balance between body and mind.

VINYASA YOGA ¡Most popular!

Also known as vinyasa flow or yoga to flow. Vinyasa yoga is a style that is characterized by the fluidity of its movements accompanied by harmonious and dynamic breathing.

In these dynamic sequences we synchronize the postures with inhalation and exhalation through “vinyasas”, which are those sequences that act as a transition between the asanas.

Vinyasa Yoga is for the type of people who like fluid movement and maintaining high energy levels. If you are looking for a more intense exercise and a practice that flows from one pose to another without stopping, this is a good option. It's also great if you like variety and creativity in your yoga practice, as the sequences can change a lot depending on the teacher.

FUNCTIONAL YOGA¡ Strengthening, strength and resistance!

Functional Yoga is an adaptation of Hatha Yoga. This modality is widely practiced in the West for its already proven benefits. To this we add “dynamic movement”, movement within the same posture, broader and global movements that integrate all the parts of the body

We thus achieve a dynamic and fluid class that allows us to develop muscular resistance, aerobic capacity, increase lung capacity and strength. Where the infinite possibilities of combinations of asanas, postures and exercises offer us the best FUNCTIONAL benefits, maintaining the essence of Yoga.

Ideal for athletes who cannot work on weights or bodybuilding and who use their own body weight with guided and professional techniques to achieve muscle strengthening and strength development.

- ROCKET YOGA ¡Fusion in movement and dynamism!

Ideal for athletes who cannot work on weights or bodybuilding and who use their own body weight with guided and professional techniques to achieve muscle strengthening and strength development.

It is great for those who want to experience the fusion between the structure of Ashtanga and the creativity of Vinyasa yoga, as it offers an energetic and fun practice.

- SLOW FLOW YOGA
¡Flow Slower!


In a typical Slow Flow class, you will practice about half of the postures you would practice in a Flow class. The rhythm is meditative and emphasizes peace and calm in the body and mind. We hold postures longer, taking several rounds of breathing in each posture, rather than moving on to each breath
